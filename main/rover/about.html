Mini Lunar Rover

2026

Overview:

The goal of this project was to make a working lunar rover that fits within a 1U CubeSat framework. This was a 1-month project for an MPowered design challenge. It took 2nd place!

About:

The scope of this design was to make a rover to land on the surface of the moon, navigate to a good spot, plant a UofM flag, and send a photo of the event back to Earth.

Specs

  • Cost: $122
  • Weight: 143.1 g
  • Features:

    • -Internal and external temperature sensors
    -Solar charging
    -Heater Coils
    -NiChrome wire release mechanism
    -6-Axis IMU
    -32Gb SD logger
    -115 kb/s LoRa antenna
    -Servo motor arm
    -External Camera
    -1.2A Dual-channel motor drivers
    -Dual-channel magnetic encoders
    -USB debugger
    -Ultra light wheels with built-in suspension
