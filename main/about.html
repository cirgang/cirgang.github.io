Chloe Irgang

Electrical Engineering Student • Embedded Systems • PCB Design

I am an electrical engineering student focused on embedded systems, custom PCB design, and autonomous robotics. I enjoy building complex hardware and firmware systems from the ground up, with an emphasis on architecture, reliability, and scalability.

Engineering Philosophy

I approach engineering from a systems-level perspective. Before designing schematics or writing firmware, I prioritize defining system architecture, power requirements, communication interfaces, and potential failure modes. This allows my designs to be robust and scalable rather than just functional.

My work emphasizes clean hardware design, modular firmware, and long-term maintainability. Whether developing an autonomous drone controller or an embedded sensing system, I focus on reliability, signal integrity, and thoughtful integration between hardware and software.

Technical Interests

  • Embedded systems and low-level firmware (C/C++)
  • Multi-layer PCB design and schematic capture
  • Autonomous drones and robotics
  • Sensor fusion and control systems
  • Real-time systems and flight control architecture

Background

I am currently studying Electrical Engineering with a focus on embedded systems, control, and autonomous hardware design. Most of my projects involve designing custom PCBs, integrating sensors and microcontrollers, and developing firmware for real-world robotic applications.

I am especially interested in building versatile, modular systems that can scale across multiple robotics platforms, including drones and autonomous robots.
